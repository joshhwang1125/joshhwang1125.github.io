ABOUT

I have always been interested in understanding how things worked. Through college, that desire manifested as my major in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, and culminated in me obtaining a master’s degree. However, I never felt that academia was my true calling, and decided to take a risk and explore while I was still young. I gained many skills in this process; I started my own business, I learned how to fix and resell electronics, and I became licensed as an insurance and financial agent. Eventually, I ended up as an IT consultant at Rand Group in Houston. Here, I was exposed to coding for the first time, and I absolutely loved it. My true passion had been discovered.



I spent a year working with the Microsoft Stack, integrating databases and building software solutions for clients. However, up to this point, I was mostly self taught, and I believed if I were to take the next step, I needed training. After researching my options, I attended App Academy, a twelve-week intensive computer programming school where I was able to dedicate 80 hours a week to learning.



Currently, I work at Brain Health Registry, which is an organization working to cure Alzheimers and other brain health diseases by acting as a patient data registry and online cognitive test platform. Since I’ve started here, I’ve led many projects which have provided a lot of value to the organization, such as creating an application to manage and track DNA spot kits, refactoring multiple features to have more robust architecture and more user friendly UI, and leading hiring efforts which has resulted in 4 engineering hires.



As a full stack developer, I have understanding of software development from the database to the UI. My current skills consists of C# (ASP.NET), SQL (Postgres, SQLite, Microsoft SQL Server), Ruby (Rails), JavaScript (jQuery, React.js), HTML and CSS, but I am largely technology agnostic. If you have a great team and are interested in connecting, feel free to reach out to me at any time. Cheers!
